// about

Who I Am

I'm a Ph.D. researcher in Mechanical Engineering (Control & Automation) at Texas A&M University, specializing in autonomous systems, robotics, and intelligent control.

My work spans motion planning, UAV scheduling, SLAM, and adaptive control — with hands-on real-time deployments on both drones and ground vehicles. I focus on building algorithms that are robust, scalable, and practical for uncertainty-heavy robotic missions in GPS-denied environments.

I've contributed to research published in IEEE Robotics & Automation Letters and AIAA SciTech, with further work currently under review at IEEE Transactions on Intelligent Transportation Systems.

Autonomous Vehicle SLAM & Control Lead

Aug 2024 – Jun 2025 · Texas A&M University

  • Led full-stack integration of localization, control, perception, and planning modules — winning 3rd place nationally among university teams across the U.S.
  • Engineered a tightly-coupled real-time SLAM pipeline fusing high-frequency IMU and Ouster LiDAR via FAST-LIO, maintaining accuracy <0.8 m over long runs.
